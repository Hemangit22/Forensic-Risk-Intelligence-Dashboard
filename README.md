Forensic Risk Intelligence: Optimizing Audit Performance and Capital Protection

🛡️ End-to-End Analytics Project: Fraud Detection & Resource Optimization
   Scale: 1,000,000+ Forensic Audit Records | Full-Stack Analytics Case Study

📖📖 Project Overview & Business Case
As enterprises scale AI-driven financial processes, traditional "Post-Audit" frameworks fail to keep pace with modern Risk Velocity. This project investigates the intersection of AI adoption and forensic risk, identifying a critical "Primary Attack Window" where 68% of incidents occur within the first 10 days—a pace that manual audits cannot match.

The Mission: Transition from reactive manual auditing to a preemptive, automated "Velocity-Gating" strategy to protect $1.19M in at-risk capital.

🎯 Project Objectives 
Derived from core forensic audit requirements, this project was built to solve four specific challenges:

1.Quantify Risk Velocity: Identify the precise temporal window (Time-to-Risk) where manual intervention fails.

2.Diagnose Operational Efficiency: Map firm-wide performance to identify "Manual Bottlenecks" where high workloads correlate with risk exposure.

3.Optimize Resource Allocation: Use a Risk-Return Frontier to determine which audit processes should be automated, maintained, or sunsetted.

4.Maximize Capital Protection: Project the financial ROI of shifting to automated "Velocity-Gating" controls.

🛠️ The Technical Workflow (Big Data Engineering)
📊 1. ETL & Data Architecture (Alteryx)
High-Volume Ingestion: Engineered a robust pipeline to ingest and normalize 1,000,000+ rows of fragmented forensic audit logs from disparate business units.

Cleansing & Transformation: Performed complex multi-join operations and null-value remediation to resolve data inconsistencies across global firm records.

Feature Engineering: Generated temporal "Maturity" flags and standardized currency headers to ensure high-fidelity inputs for downstream statistical modeling.

💾 2. Behavioral Pattern Extraction (SQL)
Temporal Analysis: Developed advanced Window Functions and CTEs to calculate "Time-to-Risk" (TTR)—measuring the delta between request initiation and incident detection.

Risk Segmentation: Created complex conditional logic to categorize incidents by "Velocity" and "Impact," identifying the critical 10-day Primary Attack Window.

Aggregation: Summarized behavioral attack patterns across the Big 4 firms to feed the Strategic Scatter Plot and identify operational outliers.

📈 3. Financial Logic & ROI Validation (Excel)
Risk-Return Frontier: Built a quantitative model to simulate the financial impact of shifting from manual labor-intensive audits to automated "Velocity-Gating."

Sensitivity Analysis: Validated a $1,192,800 capital protection opportunity by calculating the reduction in manual overhead and the prevention of high-velocity fraud losses.

Operational Benchmarking: Modeled the "Employee Workload vs. Risk Exposure" correlation to pinpoint the exact threshold where manual processes fail.

🎨 4. Executive Data Storytelling (Tableau)
Narrative Design: Translated 1M+ data points into a 4-Chapter Interactive Story that guides C-suite stakeholders from forensic discovery to strategic implementation.

Visual Intelligence: Developed a Strategic Quadrant Analysis (Scatter Plot) with custom annotations to immediately identify "Manual Bottlenecks" requiring urgent capital investment.

Dynamic UX: Implemented "High-Impact Highlighting" and drill-down tooltips to allow users to investigate specific firm-level inefficiencies (e.g., Deloitte’s workload-driven risk).


🔍 Key Strategic Findings
The 1.0-Day Gap: Analysis revealed an average 1.0-day "Time-to-Risk," proving that manual audits are too slow for modern high-velocity environments.

The 10-Day Threshold: Data-driven evidence shows that "Velocity-Gating" at the 10-day mark is the optimal point for automated intervention.

The Efficiency Paradox: High employee workloads in manual quadrants (e.g., Deloitte) directly correlate with higher risk exposure, necessitating urgent re-engineering.
